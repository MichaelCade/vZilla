vZilla Exam Plan for 2014

08/01/2014

1 Comment

 
So as I mentioned in my predictions I was going to be looking at a few certifications, something I have done each year is to set personal goals within my career, this year is no different other than I am going to share these with you.

Firstly as my role entails Pre Sales and Professional Services around Storage and Virtualisation, the exams and training I attend will co inside with that role. There are a number of vendors that I have interests in when it comes to delivering professional services. But I consider the following the order in which I am utilised by….

NetApp

VMware

Microsoft

Cisco

Veeam

I would say these are the 5 vendor areas that I dabble in, obviously not all areas. For example from a Microsoft angle I only really work with System Center 2012 and Hyper-V, everything else is a basic knowledge level, also with NetApp I currently don’t know too much about their E Series range…. Although that should change this coming year.

So my main aim within my role is NetApp, being the lead NetApp consultant I generally see all NetApp exposure that requires PS, with that in mind not only do I need to be able to deliver the solution our customers require but I also need to be able to troubleshoot and scope these implementations… with the release of Clustered OnTap many moons ago it seems now, they have also released the NCDA and NCIE exams for that same OS, my current NCIE 7 Mode expires in February 2014 so it’s a prime time to update and get the Clustered OnTap exam done, whilst away at Insight Dublin I did sit the exam but only managed a 68% score where the requirement is 75% so I am pretty confident that a sober environment could potentially be the further 7% I need to pass the exam.

Objective number 1 – NSO-504 Clustered OnTap NCIE

My second aim is to complete the upgrade process of my Microsoft MCITP Enterprise Admin cert to the MCSE 2012 Server Infrastructure. I am one exam away from completing this the 70-414. I have embarrassingly taken this exam twice and failed twice… in my defence the test centre for the first attempt had a power cut just before I sat which in turn left me waiting nearly an hour to begin the exam, but the second one was perfect conditions so I know when to admit I just wasn’t prepared enough…. So I will have to get back in the books for this one and get that passed.

Objective number 2 – Microsoft 70-414 to complete MCSE 2012 Server Infrastructure

And so the third aim and last aim from an exam point of view, this is going take a large amount of time up and is going to be pretty impossible I feel given the baby we have on the way, but it’s good to have goals right…? So firstly I am looking to complete the Cisco Data Center track to CCNP level, because I don’t hold a CCNA I will need to start by completing the CCNA – Data center this consists of 2 exams, this will then allow me to proceed to the CCNP where 5 exams are required… having sat the Cisco UCS course (DCUCI) around September 2013 after a little refresher I should be in good shape to have a go at the DCUCI and DCUCD exams. Having never sat a Cisco exam I have no experience of their questioning…. So this is going to be a new step for me as well. Not only is this going to be a completely newish area for me to dive into its also going to ask a lot of questions.. 7 exams…. Let’s see how we go.

Objective number 3 – Cisco CCNA & CCNP Data Center

So that’s my objective goals around exams for 2014, 9 exams in 12 months seems a lot and it probably is given the baby but the Microsoft and NetApp ones should be done in early 2014 leaving the full year pretty much to complete the cisco objective… it’s all going to come down to study time and what is happening with work, but as I said it’s good to have goals and objectives.

So along with the Exams, training plays a key part, I have done a lot of training courses this year mainly around the VMware stack, I have done the View ICM, vCloud ICM and View Design Best Practices, I have also been on the Cisco DCUCI course as mentioned previously. So what are my plans for training in 2014…. Well to be honest I am not really one for classroom training, I find things much easier to take in when I have small chunks to take in… so the likes of Trainsignal and CBT nuggets will most probably play a big role in 2014, especially round the cisco data center certs as they have sessions to cover all 7 exams. From a classroom point of view though I would quite like to get on the VMware SRM course to give me the full bundle… I am sure they have other courses as well… and working for a training partner I can pretty much get on the ones we run with no real issue providing I am not booked out.

One other thing I will say about training, as it comes with the job I will need to stay abreast with the latest releases from NetApp from the software suite, I will also be looking at the E Series training offerings as I believe there are not many if any PS consultants in the UK or EMEA that can deliver the E Series portfolio today.  

In conclusion to this short post…. (although second post in quick succession) I have set some lengthy training and exam goals for the coming year… but achievable! Its going to come down to my personal life as to whether completing them is completely possible in 12 months… but I am not going to be forcing myself through, I have never taken an exam and passed if I don’t actually know the content to back that up with and I don’t intend to start doing that now. A final objective of mine would be to keep up with this regular blogging also.

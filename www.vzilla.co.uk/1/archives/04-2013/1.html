AutoLab

26/04/2013

45 Comments

 
First of all I want to say a massive thank you to the guys that have created the "AutoLab" and not only that but the assistance I have received and also the level of commitment that has and must still be going into this project.

The lab can be found at http://www.labguides.com/autolab/

The creators are Alastair Cooke and Nick Marshall

http://www.demitasse.co.nz/wordpress2/

http://nickmarshall.com.au/

A detailed overview of my lab can be found on the vZilla Homelab tab at the top of the page.

When I found the AutoLab I thought brilliant no more wasting time building domain controllers and virtual centres. And with the added bonus of so many other virtual machines and nested hypervisors being stood up for no real effort.

Granted I can see where the AutoLab overlooks the step by step of install and creating new domains and the step by step process of setting up the virtual centre and so on however I do that in my every day working life all the time and there is only so many times you can stomach it…

So my reason for using AutoLab was to build the foundation I needed for the rest of my lab, the lab is mostly used for my own self training and troubleshooting however it has been known that when certain online demo centres don’t work my lab has saved the day.

My next few posts are going to be around me standing up various virtual machines and simulators, given my main focus is NetApp the first thing post will consist of the adding of both the 7mode and Clustered OnTap simulators into my lab, I will cover from download, configuring and some basic setup. Following on from that post I will move onto the installation of the NetApp management tools and then onto the Snapmanager suite (this will only consist of the products I use at the moment)

After covering the above NetApp topics I will then move into covering off Microsoft areas, these will consist of HyperV on Windows Server 2012, System Centre 2012 again on Server 2012 these will all be integrated into the AutoLab baseline, I wont at this stage be going through or providing an automation feature my skillset at the moment doesn’t contain that level of experience with either vSphere command line or powershell commands.

Then much later down the line I will be looking to introduce some Citrix Xenserver/Xendesktop integration into the lab as well as other emulators such as the Cisco UCS and Nexus 1000v

This is the first blog post on my new site, i wanted to put this on to understand where things sit etc. whilst i am here i want to express what topics i am looking at covering very briefly in this post. 

i would like to cover off firstly some personal/work goals i have for the next 12 months or at least 2013 (it has been that long since i have been trying to get this site up and running) 

i would also like to cover off my home demo lab and the reasons why? the reason why i have a home demo lab is for a number of reasons but mainly it is used for my own self training and understanding. it gives me enough exposure generally to the product sets i deal with. 

what will form most of my blog posts will be around the growth of my home demo lab and the integration with other tools/labs etc that are already out and freely available i am just adding the aspects that i require and then delving deeper into them to allow other people to use parts or all of the lab. 

along with the above topics i will also look to include and keep up to date with new features for the vendors and products that i deal with.
